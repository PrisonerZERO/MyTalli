Mobile Data — Hub & Spoke

Three mobile treatments for the Hub & Spoke pattern. The hub (aggregate dashboard) and each spoke (platform detail) need their own mobile story. Click each tab to compare.

Mobile Treatment A
Full Grid — Horizontal Scroll
Both the hub and spoke pages show their full grids on mobile. The hub has the aggregate grid, and tapping a platform navigates to the spoke where another full grid lives with tabs for Revenue/Expenses/Payouts. Everything scrolls horizontally.

Strengths

  • Full data on both hub and spoke — nothing hidden
  • Spoke page removes the Platform column (already known), slightly narrower grid
  • Back button navigation between hub and spoke feels natural on mobile

Weaknesses

  • Two pages of horizontal-scroll grids — double the pain
  • Hub grid + spoke grid = the user is scrolling sideways on every screen
  • Spoke tabs (Overview/Revenue/Expenses/Payouts) add more complexity to a small screen
  • Touch targets too small, context lost when scrolling
Verdict: Hub & Spoke makes the full-grid problem worse, not better. Now there are two levels of horizontally-scrolling tables instead of one. The spoke's extra tabs just add more tiny tap targets.

Strengths

  • Hub shows platform cards — tappable navigation to each spoke
  • Spoke shows platform-specific summary stats (Avg. Sale, Orders) before the desktop CTA
  • Platform-branded hero on spoke (Etsy orange) gives strong visual identity

Mobile Treatment C
Keyhole View
Purpose-built for both hub and spoke. The hub shows aggregate keyhole cards + platform nav. Each spoke shows platform-specific keyhole cards + a recent activity feed for that platform. No grids anywhere — just phone-native cards and lists.

Strengths

  • Hub & Spoke shines here — tapping a platform reveals a richer, platform-specific keyhole
  • Spoke has context the Dashboard+Tabs version can't: Etsy-specific stats (Avg. Sale, Orders, fee rate)
  • Recent Sales feed on the spoke is platform-filtered — no "which platform?" confusion
  • Mini charts use platform brand colors (Etsy orange bars) — reinforces where you are
  • Navigating deeper actually rewards the user with more detail, not a dead end
  • Hub platform cards double as a quick health check — see all platforms' monthly numbers at a glance

Weaknesses

  • Most development effort — hub keyhole + spoke keyhole per platform
  • No cross-platform recent activity feed (hub only shows summary, not transactions)
  • Spoke doesn't differentiate Revenue/Expenses/Payouts — just "Recent Sales"
Key Insight: Hub & Spoke + Keyhole is the strongest mobile combination. The spoke pages give Hub & Spoke a reason to exist on phones — each platform gets its own branded, focused mobile view with platform-specific stats and a filtered activity feed. Dashboard+Tabs' keyhole can only show aggregate data; Hub & Spoke's keyhole can show platform-specific data, which is exactly what "how's my Etsy doing?" users want on their phone.
