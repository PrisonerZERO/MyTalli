MyTalli Platform Approval Guide

Decline risks, pre-application requirements, and next steps for Etsy & PayPal

Last updated: March 2026

Overview

Two Platforms, Two Gates

Of the five revenue platforms MyTalli plans to integrate, two require third-party approval before we can access other merchants' data: Etsy and PayPal. Both are "approval-before-code" situations — there's no point building integrations if the gates stay closed. This document maps the risks, what could cause a decline, and what to do before applying.

These approvals are blocking. Stripe, Gumroad, and Shopify can be built without waiting for anyone. Etsy and PayPal cannot. Start these applications as early as possible — they run in parallel with development work on other platforms.

Etsy

Commercial Access — Hard Gate

Etsy requires "commercial access" approval for any app serving 4 or more shops. Without it, MyTalli is capped at 3 Etsy users. The review takes 20+ days per cycle and is approved at Etsy's sole discretion.

What Could Get Us Declined

  • Sole discretion, no published criteria. Etsy doesn't publish specific approval standards. The biggest risk is simply that they can say no — and there's no workaround or appeal path.
  • Vague or unconvincing application. The review asks for app purpose, description, and usage estimates. A generic description like "revenue dashboard" without clear value proposition or scope details invites rejection.
  • Requesting too many scopes. Asking for buyer_email or write scopes when only read access is needed signals overreach. buyer_email in particular requires a separate case-by-case approval on top of commercial access.
  • No working prototype. Applying before having a functional OAuth flow (even with 1–3 test shops) makes the app look speculative rather than real.
  • Abandoned app perception. Etsy doesn't want to approve integrations that get built and forgotten. A solo developer with no track record may raise this concern.

What to Do Before Applying

  • Register a developer account at developers.etsy.com and create a MyTalli app.
  • Build a working OAuth flow first with 1–3 test shops. Having a functional prototype strengthens the application significantly.
  • Keep scopes minimal: transactions_r and shops_r only. Do not request buyer_email initially.
  • Write a strong application description — clearly explain read-only revenue aggregation, expected shop volume, and the value to Etsy sellers.
  • Budget 20+ days per review cycle. If they come back with questions or deny, each subsequent round takes another 20+ days.

PayPal

Reporting Scopes + Partner Program — Unclear Path

PayPal has two approval layers. The first (reporting scopes) is straightforward. The second (Partner program enrollment for third-party access) is the document's biggest unknown — it's unclear whether a read-only aggregator even qualifies.

What Could Get Us Declined

  • Partner program ambiguity. It's unclear if MyTalli's use case (read-only revenue aggregation on behalf of other merchants) requires Partner enrollment. If it does, the enrollment criteria are opaque.
  • Business entity requirements. PayPal's Partner program may require a registered business entity, revenue minimums, or compliance certifications that a side project doesn't have.
  • Individual developer account. Applying as a person rather than a business entity signals "hobby project" to PayPal's review team. Third-party reporting access is an enterprise-oriented feature.
  • Scope justification. reporting/search/read on behalf of other merchants is a sensitive scope. PayPal may want to understand why an external app needs access to their users' financial transaction data.
  • No clear onboarding flow. PayPal will want to see how merchants authorize MyTalli. Having no OAuth prototype makes the application abstract.

What to Do Before Applying

  • Contact PayPal's partner team first. This is the #1 action. Ask specifically: "Does a read-only revenue aggregation app need Partner program enrollment to use reporting/search/read scope on behalf of other merchants?" Get the answer before writing any code.
  • Create a PayPal Developer account and register a MyTalli app in the Developer Dashboard.
  • Request reporting scopes (reporting/search/read, reporting/balances/read) — this is the simpler gate (24–72hr review) and unblocks sandbox testing.
  • Prepare a clear "Log In with PayPal" story — you need openid scope alongside the reporting scopes.
  • Have your LLC formed before outreach (see LLC section below).

Business Entity

Does Having an LLC Help?

Forming an LLC before applying changes how these platforms perceive MyTalli. The impact differs by platform.

🏢
PayPal — Yes, form the LLC first

PayPal's Partner program and third-party API access are geared toward businesses, not individual developers. An LLC provides a legal entity name, an EIN (tax ID) for the business PayPal account, and credibility that this is a real product. Showing up as "MyTalli LLC" instead of an individual removes one reason for them to say no on the hardest approval path.

🔧
Etsy — Helpful but not required

Etsy's developer program works fine with individual developers, and plenty of solo devs get commercial access. An LLC doesn't hurt — it signals long-term commitment and reduces the "abandoned app" concern — but don't let LLC formation delay the Etsy application. The app quality and description matter more than entity type.

Practical take: If you're planning to form an LLC for MyTalli anyway (billing, liability, tax benefits), do it before the PayPal outreach. It removes friction on the hardest approval. For Etsy, apply now regardless — don't wait for the LLC.

LLC Formation

How to Form the LLC

LegalZoom is a popular option that handles paperwork and state filing. You'll pay more than filing directly with the state, but it removes the guesswork. The EIN is free and instant from the IRS — don't pay LegalZoom for it.

Option LLC Cost EIN Cost Speed What You Get

DIY (state + IRS) State filing fee only ($50–$300) Free at irs.gov Varies (some states are same-day online) Just the filing confirmation

LegalZoom Steps

  • Select "LLC Formation" on LegalZoom.com
  • Choose your state — file in the state where you live/operate
  • Name the LLC — e.g., "MyTalli LLC" — LegalZoom checks name availability
  • Pick the basic plan — the economy tier is sufficient. Skip premium add-ons unless you want registered agent service.
  • Fill in details — your name, address, single-member LLC, member-managed
  • Operating agreement — LegalZoom generates a template. Single-member is simple but important (some banks require it to open a business account).
  • Skip the EIN add-on — do it free at irs.gov/ein once the LLC is approved. It's instant and saves $50–$100.
  • Submit & wait — LegalZoom files Articles of Organization with the state

After LLC is Approved

  • Get your EIN — free and instant at irs.gov (5 minutes)
  • Open a business bank account — bring: EIN letter, Articles of Organization, operating agreement
  • Create a business PayPal account under the LLC
  • Register the PayPal Developer app under that business account

Watch out for upsells. LegalZoom pushes registered agent service, compliance packages, and website domains at checkout. Skip these for a side project. Also note: most states require an annual report or franchise tax for LLCs ($25–$100/year) — set a calendar reminder.

Action Plan

Next Steps — In Order

These steps are sequenced by dependency and urgency. Approvals that take the longest start first. Steps that can run in parallel are noted.

  1. Contact PayPal's Partner Team

    Email or call PayPal's partner team to ask: "Does a read-only revenue aggregation app need Partner program enrollment to use reporting/search/read scope on behalf of other merchants?" This determines whether PayPal integration is even viable. Do this before any other PayPal work.

    Platform: PayPal  |  Blocker: Yes  |  Expected response: 1–2 weeks
  2. Form the MyTalli LLC

    Register the LLC, obtain an EIN, and set up a business bank account. This strengthens the PayPal application and provides the legal entity for the business PayPal Developer account. Also useful for Stripe billing, liability protection, and tax separation.

    Platform: PayPal (primary), all platforms (secondary)  |  Expected timeline: 1–3 weeks depending on state
  3. Register an Etsy Developer Account & Create App

    Sign up at developers.etsy.com and register the MyTalli app. This is a prerequisite for requesting commercial access. No approval needed for this step.

    Platform: Etsy  |  Blocker: No  |  Expected timeline: Same day
  4. Build Etsy OAuth Prototype (1–3 Test Shops)

    Implement a working OAuth flow with transactions_r and shops_r scopes. Test with 1–3 shops to prove the integration works. This prototype strengthens the commercial access application. Can run in parallel with steps 1–2.

    Platform: Etsy  |  Parallel with: Steps 1–2  |  Expected timeline: 1–2 weeks

Where to Build the Etsy Prototype

Etsy allows http://localhost redirect URIs, so the entire OAuth flow can be built and debugged locally — unlike Apple, which requires TLS-verified domains.

  • Phase 1 — Local development. Build and test the OAuth flow against https://localhost:7012. Register http://localhost:7012/signin-etsy as a redirect URI in the Etsy Developer Portal. Connect 1–3 test Etsy shops to verify the full flow end-to-end.
  • Phase 2 — Deploy to staging. Before applying for commercial access, deploy to the mytalli-web-staging Azure slot and register its URL as an additional redirect URI. This gives Etsy a live URL to evaluate during review — far more convincing than localhost screenshots.
  • Phase 3 — Production. After approval, register https://www.mytalli.com redirect URIs. The Etsy integration can ship behind a feature gate or visible only to connected users until the full Platforms page is ready.

Register all three redirect URIs (localhost, staging, production) upfront so environment switching doesn't require portal changes later.

  1. Apply for Etsy Commercial Access

    Submit the commercial access request through the Etsy Developer Portal. Include a clear description of the read-only revenue aggregation use case, expected shop volume, and link to the working prototype. Keep scopes minimal — do not request buyer_email.

    Platform: Etsy  |  Depends on: Steps 3–4  |  Review timeline: 20+ days
  2. Register a PayPal Developer App (Business Account)

    Create a PayPal Developer account under the LLC's business PayPal account. Register the MyTalli app in the Developer Dashboard. This is needed regardless of the Partner program outcome.

    Platform: PayPal  |  Depends on: Step 2 (LLC)  |  Expected timeline: Same day
  3. Request PayPal Reporting Scopes

    Request reporting/search/read and reporting/balances/read scopes through the PayPal Developer Dashboard. This is the simpler of the two gates and unblocks sandbox testing while the Partner program question is resolved.

    Platform: PayPal  |  Depends on: Step 6  |  Review timeline: 24–72 hours
  4. Pursue PayPal Partner Enrollment (If Required)

    Based on the response from step 1, either proceed with Partner enrollment or skip this step. If enrollment is required, submit the application with the LLC entity, app description, and OAuth flow documentation.

    Platform: PayPal  |  Depends on: Steps 1, 2, 6  |  Timeline: Unknown
  5. Build Stripe & Gumroad Integrations (No Approvals Needed)

    While Etsy and PayPal approvals are in progress, build the integrations that have no gates. Stripe is the richest data source and is already integrated for billing. Gumroad is the simplest API with immediate access. These ship first regardless of approval outcomes.

    Platform: Stripe, Gumroad  |  Parallel with: Steps 5–8  |  No blockers
  6. Evaluate Outcomes & Adjust Roadmap

    Once Etsy commercial access and PayPal Partner program decisions are in, adjust the platform roadmap. If either is declined, remove that platform from the launch plan and focus on the remaining four (or three). Communicate supported platforms clearly on the landing page and pricing.

    Depends on: Steps 5, 8  |  Timeline: After all decisions are in

Parallel tracks: Steps 1–2 (PayPal outreach + LLC) and steps 3–4 (Etsy prototype) can all start immediately and run in parallel. Step 9 (Stripe + Gumroad development) runs alongside everything. The goal is to have zero idle time — always building something while approvals are in flight.
